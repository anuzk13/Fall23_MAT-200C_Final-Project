A deep reading of 3D Gaussian Splatting for Real-Time Radiance Field Rendering
...

The many recent advances in neural rendering including are an interesting area for media practice. In particular because these tools have now reached a reral-time rendering standard with the paper 3D Gaussian Splatting for Real-Time Radiance Field Rendering. While previous advances in NERFs showed a vey high quality photo realistic 3D capture, NERFs cannot be easily rendered fast with high quality and therefore it seemed a bit long before we could “play” with this technologies. After the Gaussian Splatting  paper was published, it was not long before web rendering implementations were open sourced. Now, LumaAI (a 3D capture company) and Spline (a web 3D editing company) have used this implementations and offer through their platform a service to convert video into 3D splats, and also to import and render them, in combination with other 3D objects. This service is free, for now and also may not work and fail as the process of producing splats varies with video quality.

Despite (or because of this) new end user application of gaussian splatting, I was really interested in understanding the technology behind them.I jumped back and forth between reading the paper, deploying the ML models published by the Gaussian Splatting authors to create my own splats offline, and also modified the rendering code. I succeeded, and failed at all of them.

As an artifact of my final project I started this website which features:

My notes on the theoretical paper and embed some small sketches in python and p5 I made to better understand the concepts of the paper

Instructions on how to run the models (according to my experience)

A fork of the web viewer that I modified with a splat I created
